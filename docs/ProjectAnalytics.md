# Project Analytics

This document outlines the key metrics and analytics for tracking project performance, ensuring projects remain on schedule, within budget, and profitable.

## 1. Project Health

Project Health is a composite metric indicating the overall status of the project based on timeline, resource allocation, and milestone achievements.

### Key Indicators:
*   **Schedule Variance (SV):** Measures if the project is ahead of or behind schedule.
*   **Cost Variance (CV):** Measures if the project is under or over budget.
*   **Milestone Completion Rate:** The percentage of critical milestones completed on time.
*   **Risk Score:** An assessment of potential blockers or issues that could derail the project.

### Health Status Definitions:
*   🟢 **On Track (Healthy):** Project is on schedule and within budget. Risks are low and manageable.
*   🟡 **At Risk (Warning):** Minor deviations in schedule or budget. Requires attention to prevent further slippage.
*   🔴 **Off Track (Critical):** Significant delays or budget overruns. Immediate corrective action is required.

---

## 2. BOQ (Bill of Quantities) Variance

BOQ Variance tracks the difference between the estimated quantities and costs outlined in the initial Bill of Quantities versus the actual quantities consumed and costs incurred during execution.

### Key Metrics:
*   **Estimated vs. Actual Quantities:** Tracks the physical amount of materials or labor used against the baseline estimate.
*   **Estimated vs. Actual Costs:** Tracks the financial expenditure for each line item in the BOQ.
*   **Variance Percentage:** Calculated as `((Actual - Estimated) / Estimated) * 100`. A positive variance indicates an overrun, while a negative variance indicates savings.

### Analysis Focus Areas:
*   Identify line items with the highest variance.
*   Investigate root causes (e.g., price fluctuations, design changes, wastage).
*   Adjust procurement strategies or renegotiate vendor contracts based on variance trends.

---

## 3. Project Profitability

Project Profitability measures the financial success of the project by comparing the total revenue generated against the total costs incurred.

### Key Metrics:
*   **Total Revenue:** Total contract value plus any approved change orders or variations.
*   **Total Costs (COGS & Opex):** Sum of direct costs (materials, labor, subcontractors) and indirect costs (overhead, administrative expenses).
*   **Gross Profit Margin:** `((Total Revenue - Total Direct Costs) / Total Revenue) * 100`. Measures the efficiency of core project execution.
*   **Net Profit Margin:** `((Total Revenue - Total Costs) / Total Revenue) * 100`. The final bottom-line profitability after all expenses.

### Optimization Strategies:
*   Monitor scope creep to ensure all additional work is billed through change orders.
*   Optimize resource allocation to minimize downtime and labor costs.
*   Streamline procurement to secure better pricing and reduce material costs.
*   Regularly review profitability forecasts against actuals to identify and address margin erosion early.
